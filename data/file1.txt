	<h1 style="text-align:center;" class="homepage">So What Makes a New War <em>New</em>?</h1>

<p style="width:auto; text-align:right; padding-right: 9px;"><img src="http://peacemagazine.org/archive/images/118t.jpg" style="border:1px solid #99ccff; float: right; margin-left: 9px;">
<span style="font-style:italic; font-family:'Droid serif', Georgia, serif; font-size:90%;">A US airstrike on Islamic State (IS) positions in Syria, September 2014.</p><br clear="all" />

	<p>The &#8220;New Wars&#8221; of the 21st century are confounding our expectations of armed conflict. They have a different logic, and require different responses, as <strong>Mary Kaldor</strong> explains to Metta Spencer in <a href="/index.php?id=2430">New Wars and Old</a> <span class="sans"><strong><span class="caps">COMMENT</span></strong></span></p>

	<h3 class="strap">Also in this issue</h3>

	<p><a href="/index.php?id=2429">Gandhi Katha: A Life Lived According to Gita</a> <span class="sans"><strong><span class="caps">COMMENT</span></strong></span><br />
<strong>Rama Shankar Singh</strong> recalls the wisdom of Narayan Desai, the nonviolent activist who knew Gandhi well&#8212;his father Mahadev was the Mahatma&#8217;s secretary&#8212;and who died in March this year aged 90.</p>

	<p><a href="/index.php?id=2436">Pope Francis and a New Dialogue</a> <span class="sans"><strong><span class="caps">COMMENT</span></strong></span><br />
Never has a papal encyclical generated so much interest and attention as this year&#8217;s Laudato Si&#8217;. <strong>Michael Taylor Ross</strong> looks at how the Pope has worked with other faiths, scientists, and secular activists to reach a profound understanding of the relationship between faith and ecology.</p>

	<p><a href="/index.php?id=2437">Implement Canada&#8217;s Truth and Reconciliation Commission&#8217;s Recommendations</a> <span class="sans"><strong><span class="caps">COMMENT</span></strong></span><br />
<strong>Janet Nicol</strong> gives a guide to action for people who wish to learn more, or to participate more fully, in the process of reconciliation with Canada&#8217;s aboriginal peoples.</p>
